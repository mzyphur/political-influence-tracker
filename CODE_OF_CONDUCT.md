# Code of Conduct

This project adopts the
[Contributor Covenant, version 2.1](https://www.contributor-covenant.org/version/2/1/code_of_conduct/)
as its code of conduct. The canonical text is at the URL above and
is binding on every contributor, maintainer, and participant in the
project's community spaces (issue tracker, pull requests,
discussions, and any future chat / forum the project may host).

## Scope

The code of conduct applies in all project spaces and also when an
individual is officially representing the project in public spaces.
Examples of representing the project include using an official
project email address, posting via an official social media account,
or acting as an appointed representative at an online or offline
event.

## Reporting

Concerns about a contributor's behaviour, or about the maintainers'
handling of a community-conduct issue, should be reported privately
by email to:

> **mzyphur@instats.org**

Reports will be reviewed and investigated promptly and fairly, with
the privacy and security of the reporter preserved. Maintainers are
obligated to respect the privacy of anyone who reports an incident.

## Enforcement

Maintainers will follow the
[Community Impact Guidelines](https://www.contributor-covenant.org/version/2/1/code_of_conduct/#enforcement-guidelines)
in determining the consequences of a violation of this code of
conduct.

## Why a code of conduct

This project's mission is public-interest transparency. The same
discipline the project applies to its claims about Australian
political-influence records — *show the evidence, preserve the
source, and separate facts from interpretation* — applies to the
project's community as well. Contributors who can hold to that
standard are welcome regardless of background, identity, or
opinion. Contributors who can't are not.
