# Campaign Support / Party-Channelled Support

This project separates direct person-level money from campaign-support context.

## Attribution Tiers

1. `direct_person_or_group_record`
   - Source-backed gifts, benefits, interests, donations, or other records directly attached to an MP, senator, candidate, or Senate group.
   - Display rule: may be shown as direct disclosed person/group-level influence only when the source supports that scope.

2. `source_backed_candidate_or_group_campaign_record`
   - Candidate or Senate group election-return rows for donations, discretionary benefits, campaign expenditure, or return-summary context.
   - Display rule: show as campaign support connected to the candidate/group, not money personally received by an MP or senator.

3. `candidate_nil_return_with_party_branch_context`
   - A candidate return summary with nil-return status and party context.
   - Display rule: show that the candidate-level return is nil and that party annual returns are the appropriate place to inspect party-handled campaign transactions.

4. `source_backed_candidate_campaign_return`
   - A candidate election-return row linked to a House representative by exact candidate name, electorate, and state.
   - Display rule: show under "Money Connected To This Representative" as campaign support. Do not add it to the direct personal receipt total.

5. `source_backed_media_ad_activity`
   - AEC media-advertising return details.
   - Display rule: show as observed campaign advertising activity, normally linked to the advertiser/media provider unless another source supports candidate/electorate attribution.

6. `source_backed_third_party_campaign_expenditure`
   - Third-party campaign-expenditure totals from AEC election returns.
   - Display rule: show as third-party campaign activity. Do not allocate to candidates without additional evidence.

7. `inferred_party_aggregate_allocation`
   - Analytical allocation of party aggregate money to electorates/candidates.
   - Display rule: modelled estimate only. Keep separate from disclosed totals and show methods, uncertainty, and sensitivity assumptions.

## Federal AEC Basis

The AEC election-return page says candidate and Senate group returns cover donations received and electoral expenditure incurred, with nil returns due on the same election-return timetable.

For the 2025 election, the AEC reported that many candidate returns were nil and noted that endorsed candidates can report through party annual returns when a party committee handled the transactions. This is a structural disclosure feature, not automatically a missing-data bug.

Election public funding should be tracked as a separate family later: public funding generated by votes, paid to the eligible candidate or party, and not personal receipt unless the source says so. The AEC's 2025 finalised funding release reports total funding paid for that election, and the funding-rate page publishes current and historical per-vote rates.

## Current Implementation

- Backend `influence_event.event_family` now includes `campaign_support`.
- AEC election candidate/Senate group donation, benefit, expense, and summary rows are normalised.
- Candidate return rows are linked to current/past House representatives only when candidate name, electorate, and state form an exact unique match. Current links are labelled as name/electorate context without a temporal-office check until historical candidate and office-term dating is expanded.
- Senate records remain at state/group/party context unless a source supports senator-specific attribution.
- API representative profiles expose `campaign_support_summary`, `campaign_support_recent_events`, and a caveat separate from direct person records.
- The frontend displays a separate "Money Connected To This Representative" panel and keeps direct reported totals separate from campaign-support totals.
